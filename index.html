Recursive Pairs Trading
Agentic AI · Live Research System

An agent that runs the trade, grades its own thesis, and rewrites the rulebook.

This is a real statistical-arbitrage research pipeline. Daily universe scans, cointegration screens, risk gates, position sizing are operated end-to-end by Claude as the autonomous agent. It isn't a chatbot bolted onto a spreadsheet, it's a closed loop. The system trades a hypothesis, logs why every position won or lost, and periodically audits that log against its own rules. When the evidence contradicts the rulebook, the rulebook changes.

The thesis

A niche sized for what it actually is

Small/mid-cap illiquid pairs sit below institutional radar and above retail noise. Too small for large funds to bother with, too slow (5–10 day holds) for retail momentum chasers. That same thinness caps how much capital the strategy can actually absorb before its own trading moves the names it depends on. The design goal was never unlimited scale; it was a dedicated book sized to the edge that actually exists.

01 · The daily loop

Seven steps, run without a human in most of them

Each trading day the same pipeline runs autonomously. A person approves the entries and exits it recommends. Everything upstream of that decision is the agent working through tools, pulling data, screening candidates, checking its own rules, and writing down what happened.

01

Refresh the universe

Nightly filter of the liquid small/mid-cap equity universe on market cap, dollar volume, and price floors. Whole sectors are excluded at this stage. Anything with binary, unhedgeable event risk never reaches the scan.

02

Scan for pairs

Cointegration test, Hurst exponent, and half-life estimate across every candidate pair in the universe. Output is a ranked list of statistically mean-reverting spreads, not a single "best" pick.

Deep-dive check

Rolling-window stability, hedge-ratio drift, sector-beta regression against the relevant sector ETF. Scored as a confidence spectrum, not a pass/fail. See the risk framework below for why.

Autonomous

Risk gate

Earnings and dividend check across a fallback cascade of data sources (detail below), sector concentration check, and a hard-kill screen for disguised directional bets.

Autonomous
05

Size the trade

Position sized off the OLS hedge ratio between the two legs, never volatility-weighted independently, which would quietly break the hedge and turn a pairs trade into two separate directional bets.

Monitor & decide

Daily re-check of z-score, cointegration, and time held versus half-life. Recommends hold, take-profit, stop-loss, or time-stop exit. A person confirms the action.

Every close, win, loss, or scratch, is recorded with its exit reason. This log is the raw material for step three's rules on the next cycle.

The loop closes on itself. Step seven's log doesn't just sit in an archive. It periodically gets read back against the rules used in steps three and four. That read-back is where this stops being a script and starts being an agent with memory. Section two below is what that looks like in practice.
02 · The recursive part

When the evidence contradicted the rulebook

This is a generalized account of a real recalibration the system went through, a pattern that has repeated more than once as the strategy accumulated trades. Specific figures and dates are omitted; the structure of the correction is not.

Original rule

Entry required a candidate pair to pass four simultaneous structural checks, long-sample cointegration, high rolling stability, tight hedge-ratio drift, and low sector-beta exposure, all at once, or the trade was rejected outright.

The checks were borrowed from a longer-horizon arbitrage playbook, multi-year structural stability as the bar for "this relationship is real."

Rewritten rule

Most checks became a graduated confidence score that sets position conviction, not a gate. Only checks that predict short-horizon risk stayed as hard kills, sector-beta exposure past a fixed threshold, extreme hedge-ratio instability, and a small set of structurally excluded sectors.

The real risk control moved to a time-based exit. Leave the trade if it hasn't converged within roughly twice its statistical half-life, regardless of how it scored at entry.

The diagnosis, in the agent's own reasoning
Running the original four-check gate back against a batch of closed trades showed it rejecting winners at almost the same rate as losers. The check couldn't tell them apart. The mismatch was the holding period. This strategy exits in five to ten trading days, but the rejected checks were designed to certify multi-year structural stability. A pair doesn't need three years of stability to be tradable for two weeks; it needs the current regime to hold that long. Once that distinction was explicit, the fix followed directly. Keep the checks that catch a trade turning into a disguised directional bet, drop the ones measuring the wrong time horizon, and let the clock, not the entry gate, be the primary risk control.
03 · Risk framework

Most rules are a spectrum. A few aren't.

The recalibration above changed how most checks are treated, but a handful of conditions still end a trade outright, no matter how good the surrounding stats look.

CheckTreatmentReasoning
Rolling stability Spectrum Scales conviction and position sizing rather than blocking entry. Low stability is normal for a regime-dependent, short-hold pair.
Hedge-ratio drift Spectrum Moderate drift is expected and tolerated; only extreme drift downgrades or kills a candidate.
Sector-beta exposure Hard kill Past a fixed ratio, the "pairs trade" is no longer market-neutral. It's a directional sector bet wearing a stats costume.
Sub-sector driver mismatch Hard kill Same industry label, different economics, like two REIT sub-types whose demand drivers move in opposite directions on the same rate cycle.
Binary event risk Excluded upstream Certain sectors are removed at the universe stage. A single overnight event can gap either leg 30–80%, which no statistical edge survives.
Time in trade vs. half-life Hard exit rule The one rule that never got softened. Statistical edge decays with time; holding past the time stop is exposure to sector noise, not the trade thesis.
04 · Tool orchestration

Any one data source will eventually lie to you

The single highest-stakes check in the whole pipeline is also the least reliable, whether either leg of a pair reports earnings before the position would exit. One bulk data source, checked once, isn't enough.

Per-ticker, source D

This cascade exists because of a near-miss, not a design review. A position was nearly held straight through an earnings print that the primary bulk source simply didn't return. The same endpoint could show a ticker clean on one call and flag it on the next. The fix wasn't "trust the API more." It was to chain five independent lookups and treat any disagreement between them as a block on the trade, not a coin flip to resolve.

05 · What this is actually demonstrating

Translating the trading system into agentic AI capability

The strategy is the vehicle. The properties below are the point.

Autonomous multi-step orchestration

Seven dependent stages, data pull, screen, risk check, sizing, monitoring, logging, chained without a human authoring each individual step.

Persistent memory, not a stateless chat

A living rules document the agent reads at the start of every session and edits when evidence warrants it. Context that survives across days, not a conversation that resets.

Empirical self-correction

Rules changed because the agent's own trade log contradicted them, not because a human noticed a bug and patched it by hand.

Risk-gated autonomy, human in the loop

The system proposes; a person confirms every entry and exit. Autonomy scoped to analysis and monitoring, not unsupervised execution.

Resilience across unreliable tools

Real external data sources are inconsistent. The pipeline is built to degrade gracefully across five fallbacks rather than trust any single one.

Judgment over hardcoding

Most checks became graduated scores instead of pass/fail gates, closer to how a human analyst weighs conflicting evidence than to a rigid rules engine.

04 · Why banks, and why that's the trap

The best stats in the universe were also the most correlated

Regional bank pairs consistently produced the cleanest cointegration and the most reliable reversion. The natural response was to lean into what worked. But concentrating in the sector with the best per-pair statistics is exactly how a "diversified" book of 17 tickers ends up moving like one position.

Diversification still did something real. The theoretical sum of each pair's standalone volatility was roughly $130K annualized; the realized portfolio volatility came in around $52K, a genuine ~60% reduction from holding pairs that don't move in lockstep. But +0.10 average correlation is not zero, and in a book leaning almost entirely regional-bank, it compounds into the KRE-sized number above.

05 · The honest read

Not a broken edge. An unpriced one.

The idiosyncratic mean-reversion signal is real. The 60% per-pair win rate and the trades that converged on schedule show that. What wasn't real was the assumption that hedging each pair individually added up to a hedged book. It didn't, because the same sector kept supplying the best-looking trades.

What changed as a result. The risk framework moved from managing pair count to managing net book-level residual beta. New candidates are now scored on whether they add to or offset the book's existing KRE exposure, not just on their own stats. Diversifying into uncorrelated, non-bank sectors became an explicit goal rather than a nice-to-have. That change traces directly back to the rule-rewrite pattern on the System page. This is the evidence that drove one.
06 · What this is actually demonstrating

Reporting the result plainly is the skill

Not the trading performance. The diagnosis.

Hidden factor discovery

Computing a book-level exposure across 17 positions that no single pair's hedge ratio would ever reveal on its own.

Signal vs. noise, separated

Distinguishing genuine idiosyncratic reversion (real, per-pair) from correlated sector swings (the actual source of the drawdown) instead of conflating the two.

Turning an uncomfortable result into a rule

The residual-beta finding became a scoring input for every new trade, not a footnote explained away after the fact.

Reporting the number, not the narrative

Net result, roughly breakeven, slightly negative, over seven weeks. Stated plainly, with the mechanism that explains it, rather than a cherry-picked winning stretch.
